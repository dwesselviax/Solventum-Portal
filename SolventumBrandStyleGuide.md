# Solventum Brand Style Guide

## Overview
The Solventum brand balances humanity and credibility through a carefully curated visual system. Our primary color is green—representing the color of life—with a vibrant bright green capturing imaginative energy and a rich dark green showing our caring, people-first nature.

### Color Usage Principles

**Primary Color Ratio:**
- Prioritize white and dark green with slightly reduced brand green
- Consistent across print and digital applications

**Secondary Color Usage:**
- Used with less frequency
- Add dimension and variety
- Work well for backgrounds and content blocking
- Support product photography

**Color Restrictions:**
- ❌ Do not use colors outside the brand palettes
- ❌ Do not create gradients from palette colors
- ❌ Do not use tints or transparency with brand colors
- ❌ Do not use type/background combinations that aren't ADA-compliant
- ❌ Do not use data visualization colors for typography
- ❌ Do not use multiple colors within one sentence
- ❌ Do not use expanded digital interface palette for charts/graphs

## Typography

### Primary Font: Solve Pro

A customized sans serif designed to balance precise forms with moments of humanity and approachability. Features sharp edges and dynamic curves for functional, legible letterforms.

**Available Weights (with Italic variants):**
- Light (300)
- Regular (400)
- Medium (500)
- Bold (700)
- Extra Bold (800)

**Font Formats Available:**
- **OTF (OpenType Font):** Desktop version with all advanced features; best for design applications
- **TTF (TrueType Font):** Simplified desktop version; best for office applications
- **Web (WOFF/WOFF2):** For web development

**Design Philosophy:**
- Precise cuts and formations
- Dynamic curves
- Geometric shapes
- Open and geometric letterforms
- Increased legibility

### System Font: Arial

Used for business communications only:
- Microsoft 365 documents
- Email signatures
- CAD files

Arial ensures consistency across workstations and operating systems.

**Important:** Do not embed fonts into PowerPoint presentations.

### CCJK Language Font: Noto Sans

For Simplified Chinese, Traditional Chinese, Japanese, and Korean languages (not supported by Solve Pro).

**Available variants:**
- Noto Sans Simplified Chinese
- Noto Sans Traditional Chinese
- Noto Sans Japanese
- Noto Sans Korean

Open font license—free for any use.

## Digital Type Hierarchy

**Structure:** 6 heading levels (H1–H6) + body styles + eyebrow styles

### Digital Type Styles

| Style | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| H1 | Largest heading, page title |
| H2 | Major section heading |
| H3 | Subsection heading |
| H4 | Minor subsection heading |
| H5 | Small subsection heading |
| H6 | Smallest heading |
| Body Bold | Emphasized body text |
| Body Regular | Standard body copy |
| Eyebrow Bold | Small, emphasized label text |
| Eyebrow Regular | Small, regular label text |

### Digital Type Hierarchy Principles

- **Clear Organization:** Shows headings and important information clearly
- **Emphasis:** Highlights essential points
- **Readability:** Ensures legibility across all devices, including mobile
- **Visual Appeal:** Creates consistent, pleasing layouts
- **Branding:** Maintains brand identity consistency
- **Accessibility:** Helps screen readers interpret content for visually impaired users
- **Inclusivity:** Supports neurodivergent audience accessibility

## Print Type Hierarchy

*Guidelines based on 8.5" × 11" documents*

### Hierarchy Principles

- Use different font sizes and weights to establish visual hierarchy
- Larger type first—introduces content
- Smaller body copy—heart of information
- Consistent fonts and styles create recognizable brand identity
- Contrast of large and small type communicates hierarchy
- Left-align all type

### Type Color Guidelines

**Preferred Combinations:**
- Dark Green on White
- White on Dark Green
- Brand Green for visual emphasis

### Special Typographic Treatments

#### Emphasizing Important Sentences
Use color to highlight important ideas or calls to action. Follow these rules:

- **Do:** Highlight entire line of text if idea fits on one line
- **Do:** Recolor a whole sentence
- **Do:** Emphasize single word within one sentence
- **Don't:** Break single sentences with multiple colors

#### Color-Changing Headlines
Emphasize heroic statements with color transitions.

**Rules:**
- Reserve for dark green backgrounds only
- Best for 1–5 word statements per line
- Use 2–4 lines maximum
- **Do:** Arrange colors from darkest to lightest

**Recommended Color Sequences:**

*Two lines on dark green:*
1. Brand Green
2. White

*Three lines on dark green:*
1. Teal
2. Medium Teal
3. Brand Green

*Four lines on dark green:*
1. Teal
2. Medium Teal
3. Brand Green
4. Light Teal

*Two lines on light teal:*
1. Dark Green
2. Hyperlink Teal

#### Emphasizing Keywords
**Option 1 - Heavier Font Weight:**
Use bolder weight for keyword emphasis within a sentence.

**Option 2 - Enlarged Text:**
Use larger text on a different line (fleet and environmental office branding only—not digital).

### Typography Don'ts

- ❌ Do not scale type disproportionately
- ❌ Do not use type/background combinations that aren't AA-compliant
- ❌ Do not skew or distort type
- ❌ Do not use Noto Sans for languages available in Solve Pro
- ❌ Do not use typefaces outside the brand system
- ❌ Do not add special effects to type
- ❌ Do not use overly tight or loose leading
- ❌ Do not use overly loose tracking
- ❌ Do not use multiple colors within one word
- ❌ Do not use multiple colors within one sentence
- ❌ Do not ignore ADA compliance
- ❌ Do not use color-changing headlines on white backgrounds
- ❌ Do not change color text within paragraphs (headlines only)
- ❌ Do not use color-changing headlines on more than 4 lines
- ❌ Do not change weight/size when changing color per idea
- ❌ Do not break up ideas using color
- ❌ Do not use italics in headlines or long paragraphs
- ❌ Do not overuse italics (difficult for neurodivergent users)

## Color & Typography Combinations

### ADA Accessibility Compliance

Only these color combinations are approved for typography and pass WCAG AA-accessibility standards:

**Recommended Combinations:**
- Dark Green text on White background
- White text on Dark Green background
- Brand Green text on White background (use sparingly)
- Digital Text Gray on Light Gray background
- Dark Green on Light Teal background

**Note:** WCAG compliance applies to online text only—not artwork or printed materials.

**Caution:** Exercise caution with bright green on dark green combinations. Avoid large blocks of brightly colored text.
